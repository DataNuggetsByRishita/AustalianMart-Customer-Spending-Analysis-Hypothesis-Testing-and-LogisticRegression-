# AustalianMart-Customer-Spending-Analysis-Hypothesis-Testing-and-LogisticRegression-
Project Overview

This project analyzes survey responses from 400 AustralianMart customers to understand spending behavior, discount usage patterns, and loyalty program participation. The analysis combines statistical testing and predictive modeling to uncover meaningful customer segments and support evidence-based marketing decisions.

The objective was to translate customer data into actionable insights that enhance targeting, promotional strategy, and retention initiatives.

Business Objective

Identify key drivers of customer spending behavior

Evaluate the impact of discounts and loyalty program participation

Compare purchasing patterns across age groups and gender

Support data-driven customer segmentation strategies

Provide statistical evidence to guide marketing investments

Methodology
1. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

Demographic distribution analysis

Spending pattern visualization

Discount usage and loyalty participation trends

2. Statistical Analysis

Hypothesis testing to compare spending across demographic groups

Significance testing to validate observed differences

Sample size estimation to ensure statistical reliability

3. Predictive Modeling

Logistic Regression to model likelihood of loyalty participation or spending behavior

Model interpretation to identify significant predictors

Key Insights

The analysis provided:

Clear differences in spending behavior across age and gender segments

Quantified impact of discount usage on purchase frequency

Evidence-based recommendations for targeted marketing campaigns

Insights into which customer groups are most responsive to loyalty initiatives

Business Impact

The findings enable AustralianMart to:

Refine customer segmentation strategies

Optimize discount allocation

Improve loyalty program targeting

Strengthen data-backed marketing decision-making
